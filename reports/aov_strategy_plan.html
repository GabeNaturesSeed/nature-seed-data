The Opportunity Map

Most customers currently buy a single product in one size. The goal is to move them to either (a) a larger size of the same product, (b) a companion add-on that ships free alongside, or (c) a cross-category product relevant to their land use. None of these tactics require new product development — they all use existing SKUs.

Tactic Overview

Priority 1

🚢 Free Shipping Capacity Bar (Cart)

Show customers how many lbs they're using of their free 125-lb shipping window. "You have X lbs in your cart — you can add up to Y more lbs for free." This is different from a dollar threshold bar — it uses lb weight as the lever since the policy is weight-based.

Expected AOV lift: +$18–35 per triggered order
WooCommerce: Custom notice widget or WPC Product Bundles + cart hook
Priority 1

🌱 Checkout Cross-Sell Add-Ons

At cart/checkout, show 2–3 complementary lightweight add-ons based on what's in the cart. E.g., a lawn seed buyer sees "Add Micro Clover + Inoculant to complete your lawn." Each add-on adds 5–10 lbs at $40–75. Target: 25% attach rate.

Expected AOV lift: +$15–40 at 25% attach
WooCommerce: CartFlows upsell step, or WooCommerce Cart Add-ons plugin
Priority 1

📦 Size Tier Upgrade Nudge (Product Page)

On every product page, show coverage area per size tier: "5 lb covers 500 sq ft • 10 lb covers 1,000 sq ft • 25 lb covers 2,500 sq ft." Add "Most customers choose 10 lb" social proof badge on the middle tier. Show savings per 1,000 sq ft to make larger size look like better value.

Expected AOV lift: +$20–50 per upgrade from 5→10 lb or 10→25 lb
WooCommerce: Variation swatches plugin with custom description per variation
Priority 2

🤝 "Complete Your Kit" Cart Bundle

When a customer adds lawn seed to cart, show a cart notice: "Complete your lawn setup — add Tackifier + Fertilizer for $XX more (ships free)." Pre-built kit bundles at a slight discount vs. buying individually. No new product creation needed — just virtual bundle SKUs.

Expected AOV lift: +$40–80 per kit conversion at 15% attach
WooCommerce: WPC Frequently Bought Together, or Composite Products
Priority 2

🔄 "Frequently Bought Together" on Product Pages

Amazon-style FBT section on every product page. Show 2–3 specific companion products with combined price and 1-click "Add All to Cart." Must be hand-curated per product category — automated recs will show irrelevant products.

Expected AOV lift: +$15–30 per conversion at 10–15% attach
WooCommerce: WPC Frequently Bought Together plugin (free tier works)
Priority 2

📐 Coverage Area Calculator (Product Page)

Simple JS widget: "How much area do you need to cover?" → enter sq ft → auto-suggests the right size and quantity. Customers who use this calculator convert at higher rates AND buy larger sizes. Bonus: captures intent data.

Expected AOV lift: +$25–60 for undersized cart correction
Custom JS widget (simple HTML/CSS/JS, can inject via WooCommerce shortcode)
Priority 3

📧 Klaviyo Post-Purchase Upsell Sequence

Timed email sequence triggered on purchase: Day 3 = application tips (educational, no pitch). Day 14 = "Any thin spots? Add X" (reorder + add-on). Day 45 = "Time to overseed?" (seasonal reorder). Day 90 = cross-category suggestion based on purchase type.

Expected additional revenue: $2–5 RPR on 2nd-purchase customers
Klaviyo flows — improve existing Upsell Flow (see flow assessment)
Priority 3

🎁 Loyalty Volume Reward (No Discount)

Instead of discounting, offer a free add-on at a spend threshold. "Add Micro Clover ($44.99 value) FREE when you reach $150 in your cart." This raises AOV without margin erosion and ships within the free-shipping lb window.

Expected AOV lift: +$30–50 to reach threshold (no margin erosion)
WooCommerce: AutomateWoo free gift rule, or WooCommerce Gift With Purchase plugin

Free Shipping Capacity Bar — Implementation Spec

1
Trigger: Show in cart sidebar and cart page when cart weight is between 5 lbs and 110 lbs. Hide when cart is empty or over 115 lbs (close to limit).
2
Message format: "You're shipping [X] lbs — you can add up to [125-X] more lbs for free! [Suggested add-on: Micro Clover adds just 1 lb for $44.99]"
3
Visual: Green progress bar from 0 → 125 lbs. At 80–90% of limit, show orange warning: "You're close to the free shipping limit — add lightweight items only."
4
Technical: WooCommerce provides cart weight via WC()->cart->get_cart_contents_weight(). Can be output via a small PHP snippet in functions.php or via a child theme cart hook. No plugin required.
5
Add-on suggestions in bar: Show product thumbnail + name + weight + price for 1 pre-selected add-on. Add-on should be the highest-margin, lowest-weight item appropriate for the cart contents (S-MICRO for lawn carts, S-INNOC for pasture/clover carts, S-RICE for wildflower carts).

Size Tier Upgrade — Product Page Spec

1
Coverage table below variation selector: three columns — Size | Coverage Area | Price per 1,000 sq ft. Highlight the middle tier as "Best Value."
2
Social proof badge: "🌱 Most customers choose [10 lb]" as a small label on the recommended size. This nudges fence-sitters to the higher size without a discount.
3
Savings callout: "Save $X.XX per 1,000 sq ft vs. the 5 lb bag" shown next to the 10 or 25 lb size. Makes the upgrade feel economically rational.
4
Coverage seeding rates: Use 5–7 lbs per 1,000 sq ft for lawn seed, 10–15 lbs per acre for pasture, 2–4 lbs per 1,000 sq ft for wildflower blends as baseline. These are standard industry rates.
