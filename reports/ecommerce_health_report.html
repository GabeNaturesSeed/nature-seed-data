MTD Highlights
⚡
Revenue down MTD, driven entirely by AOV compression
March 1-10 revenue is -8.7% YoY ($121,983 vs $133,652). Orders actually grew +24% (900 vs 727), but average order value fell -26% to $136 from $184. Smaller basket size, not less traffic, is the story.
📊
Marketing efficiency held — MER flat YTD, ROAS improved
YTD MER is identical at 4.43x vs 4.43x — every dollar of ad spend produces the same total revenue return. ROAS improved from 2.42x to 3.09x, suggesting better conversion quality on Google. The spend reduction (-28%) explains most of the YTD revenue gap.
💡
YTD revenue decline correlates 1:1 with reduced ad spend
YTD revenue is -28.5% — and ad spend dropped -28.5%. January and February 2026 were run at significantly lower spend (Jan: $14,271 vs $44,151; Feb: $45,663 vs $48,374). This was a budget decision, not a market efficiency loss.
🛒
Walmart launched — new incremental revenue channel
$3,486 Walmart revenue in March MTD, adding 2.9% on top of WooCommerce. Zero Walmart revenue existed in 2025 — all incremental. Full-month March 2025 delivered $0 from Walmart.
🌱
Spring 2026 ramp looks solid — March daily rate on pace
Current March MTD daily rate: $12,198/day. March 2025 full-month average: $14,502/day. At current pace, March 2026 projects to ~$378,147 — vs actual $449,562 in March 2025. Spring acceleration is tracking similarly to last year.
